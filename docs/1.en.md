## 1. Executive Summary

Traditional tokenomics suffer from a fundamental flaw: unbounded downside risk. In standard DeFi models, price relies entirely on market sentiment and "mercenary" liquidity providers who flee at the first sign of volatility.

`TMCTOL` (Token Minting Curve + Treasury-Owned Liquidity) is a paradigm shift from event-driven blockchain logic to a `Token-Driven Economic Automaton`. It is a self-contained economic machine that replaces faith-based stability with mathematically defined boundaries.

The system combines unidirectional token emission (TMC) with automated treasury accumulation (TOL) to create a `calculable price floor ranging from 11% to 25%` of the equilibrium price. This structure transforms the asset class from a purely speculative vehicle into a "Rising Stability Asset", where volatility is harvested to mechanically elevate the price floor over time.

## 2. The Core Problem: Unbounded Risk

In current DeFi architectures, liquidity is rented, not owned.

1.  `The Liquidity Trap`: Protocols pay high inflation rewards to attract liquidity. When rewards dry up, liquidity leaves, causing a death spiral.
2.  `Zero Floor`: In an XYK pool (Uniswap V2 style), if users sell 100% of the supply, the price asymptotically approaches zero. There is no mathematical backstop.
3.  `Governance Fragility`: "One token, one vote" systems are vulnerable to flash-loan attacks and lack long-term alignment.

`The TMCTOL Solution`:
Instead of renting liquidity, the protocol `captures` it. By routing 66.6% of all minting revenue directly into permanent Protocol-Owned Liquidity (POL), TMCTOL ensures that every token in circulation is backed by a growing reserve of hard assets.

## 3. Technical Architecture: The Economic Automaton

TMCTOL operates as a deterministic state machine. It abandons the traditional "Request-Response" model in favor of `Continuous Flow Processing`. The network coordinates through explicit, permissionless token flows between dedicated actors.

### 3.1 The Actor Constellation

The system is composed of specialized actors that enforce economic invariants via Substrate hooks (`on_initialize`, `on_finalize`):

- `TMC Actor (The Ceiling)`: Manages the linear minting curve. It allows infinite upside but strictly regulates entry price.
- `TOL Actor (The Floor)`: Automatically zaps 66.6% of incoming capital into XYK liquidity positions. This liquidity is owned by the protocol, not users.
- `Axial Router (The Engine)`: An intelligent aggregator that directs trades to either the TMC (minting) or XYK pools (secondary market) based on pure economic efficiency.
- `Burning Manager (The Sink)`: A "black hole" for fees. It aggregates dust and 0.5% router fees, converting them to Native tokens and burning them to compress supply.

`Key Property: The "Omnivorous" Machine`
The system utilizes "Balance-Delta Verification." It does not trust the return values of external calls; it verifies the physical change in ledger balances. This makes the system immune to flash-loan price manipulation and allows it to process "erroneous" transfers as valid economic contributions.

## 4. Mathematical Foundations

The security of TMCTOL is not economic—it is algebraic.

### 4.1 The Deterministic Ceiling

The entry price is defined by a linear slope, ensuring no front-running is possible during minting:

$$P_{ceiling}(S) = P_0 + \frac{m \cdot S}{PRECISION}$$

Where $P_0$ is the initial price, $m$ is the slope, and $S$ is the current supply.

### 4.2 The Hyperbolic Floor

The floor price is derived from the immutable reserves held in the TOL (Treasury-Owned Liquidity). Even in a catastrophic scenario where 100% of the user-held supply is sold, the constant product formula ($x \cdot y = k$) guarantees the price cannot drop below a specific non-zero value:

$$P_{floor} = \frac{R_{foreign}}{(R_{native} + S_{sold})^2}$$

This guarantees a floor protection range of `11% (minimum)` to `25% (maximum)` of the ceiling price, depending on the deployment of liquidity buckets.

### 4.3 The Ratchet Effect (Bidirectional Compression)

The system implements a "virtuous cycle" mechanism driven by volatility.

1.  `Fee Collection`: 0.5% of every trade is collected by the Router.
2.  `Supply Burn`: These fees are used to buy back and burn Native tokens.
3.  `Dual Impact`:
    - `Ceiling Drops`: Lower Supply ($S$) $\rightarrow$ Lower Mint Price.
    - `Floor Rises`: Same Reserves / Lower Supply $\rightarrow$ Higher Floor Price per token.

This results in `Spread Compression`. The ceiling and floor converge toward an equilibrium price ($P_{eq}$), trapping value within the system.

$$P_{eq} \approx \sqrt{\frac{R_{TOL} \times m}{PRECISION}}$$

## 6. The Axial Router & Fee Mechanics

The `Axial Router` is the only entry point for trading. It abstracts "Path Discovery" from "Mechanism Selection".

### 6.1 Intelligent Routing

When a user wants to buy, the Router calculates an `Efficiency Score` comparing:

1.  `TMC Minting`: Creating new tokens (if the market price is high).
2.  `XYK Swap`: Buying from the pool (if the market price is low).

It automatically routes the trade through the cheapest path. This creates an internal arbitrage loop that feeds the protocol.

### 6.2 Deflationary Engine

- `Router Fee`: 0.5% on all swaps.
- `Destination`: 100% of fees are sent to the `Burning Manager`.
- `Execution`: Fees are accumulated and burned in batches to optimize gas. This constant buy-pressure acts as the "fuel" for the Floor Elevation Velocity.

### 6.3 Security

The Router uses a `TVL-Weighted EMA with Half-Life Decay`. This prevents "sandwich attacks" by ensuring the internal oracle price cannot be manipulated instantaneously within a single block.

## 7. L2 Governance & Security

The system introduces `GovXP` (Governance Experience Points) to transition from plutocracy to meritocracy.

### 7.1 Declining Voting Power

To prevent "flash-governance" attacks where whales vote at the last second:

- `Start of Vote`: 10x Voting Power.
- `End of Vote`: 1x Voting Power.
  This linear decay forces large holders to signal their intent early.

### 7.2 GovXP (Soulbound Reputation)

Active participants earn GovXP, which provides a multiplier (up to 5x) on their voting weight and staking rewards. This shifts influence toward competent actors rather than just capital holders.

### 7.3 L1 Native Protection

L1 Treasury holdings in L2 protocols receive a `constant 10x voting multiplier` that does not decay. This acts as a "NATO Article 5" defense mechanism—the L1 ecosystem can step in to veto malicious proposals in any L2 project.

### 7.4 Anti-MEV Streaming

Large treasury transfers are executed via `DripVaults`, which stream funds per-block rather than in lump sums. This eliminates MEV (Miner Extractable Value) opportunities.
